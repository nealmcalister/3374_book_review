The People's Platform

Taking Back Power and Culture in the Digital Age

A Collaborative Book Review

Astra Taylor’s The People’s Platform: Taking Back Power and Culture in the Digital Age warns a culture consumed by digital media that the advancements and conveniences offered are not what they seem. Taylor illustrates how Web 2.0 influences the ways Internet giants, artists, and users are taken advantage of. The cost this bestows upon digital culture is enormous. These hidden costs refer to the self-esteem, attention, privacy, and knowledge that individuals relinquish when they engage in free digital media (Taylor 261).

After discussing the multitudinous problems currently plaguing digital media consumed culture, Taylor’s trade book employs an urgent tone. She encourages participants in digital culture to democratize, revise copyright laws, and produce sustainably. This trade book, replete with insight into problems as well as progressive solutions proves relevant to educators and students alike who often engage in this culture consumed by digital media. As digital media becomes more prominently woven into classrooms, Taylor points out relevant concerns as well as sensible solutions, making The People’s Platform essential to understanding the 21st century’s cultural implications.
